About me






I am Sebastiaan Van Hoecke and I study Applied Informatics at Ghent. My passion goes out to GNU/Linux and networking as you will notice.

I am 21 years old and live in Dendermonde.

Next to school I play competitive table tennis, every week I go out with a solid group of friends, I listen to electronic music and to stay healthy I run three times a week and do some workout.

Interests




  • GNU / Linux is life

    My main interest is Linux, I fell in love with it in the year of 2014. I got so annoyed by Windows that I decided to try something else, I installed Ubuntu. It was the missing piece my laptop needed to be perfect. Now I roll Fedora and use the command line like as it was always there. I have experience with Fedora, Ubuntu, CentOS, Kali, Arch, Solus, Elementary, Linux mint and so on.

    Free as in freedom

    With Linux the passion for FLOSS (free/libre and open-source software) also grew, a great man once said "software should be free as in freedom" and he has a point. In my opinion a lot more software should have the GNU General Public License. The beauty of this is that it's really happening, more and more software companies are taking the open-source road, it's not free/libre software yet but it's a start.


    The Networker

    Next to Linux my other passion is networking and everything that it includes, from setting up a simple server to configuring vlans, from capturing packages with wireshark to network policies in mandatory access controls. I always had a great interest in the internet, how does it work, who controls it, and so on. Now I realize the internet is nothing more than 0.0.0.0/0 with 4 bilion addresses.

  • Design the Internet

    A bit of a bad side at me is that I can take my time to design, to pick a color, to get the right font, to move an object from one side to the other, ... Not only I like to design the web, I also like to design my desktop environment or things in real life.

    White hat hacker

    A dream of mine is that one day I will master Kali Linux, Metasploit, NitroZeus, Social Engineering Toolkit and so on. When I read articles about how someone manages to hack whole companies, or when I read about honeypots and rootkits I always feel a bit sedated. I want to know how they did it, of course I would use this in a good way.

    Technology builder

    At home I have a lot of hardware parts, I love to build something that works out of broken parts for example I've build a server from laptop parts that I can put up against the wall. Disassemble laptops till the smallest parts and putting it all together again satisfies me. Knowing your hardware that you use everyday is knowing yourself.

Skills



I know Linux

I've taught myself the basics and the advanced stuff of Linux, I learn new things about Linux everyday. I've read books like; The Linux Bible, TLCL, Linux Fundamentals. I have experience with docker and LXC, CoreOS is also known to me. Mandatory access controls isn't a pain in the ass anymore. I have knowledge about PAM and kernel hardening, securing servers or setting up servers and make them communicate with each other is well known to me. On my github platform you will find guides about Linux and Linux security.



Virtualization, orchestration and networking

I have skills in virtualization with kvm, vmware and virtualbox. I also have experience with cloud computing, deploying multiple servers on Azure or Digitalocean isn't a problem. The tools I use the most for virtualization are Vagrant, Docker and of course shell scripting. Connecting servers or using protocols to send data between servers is not hard anymore. I also have my own homeservers where I test stuff on.

Penetration testing

Each network needs to be secure, but how secure is secure? I want to know against what I need to defend myself, so I try to beat the hacker and hack things which I am allowed to hack legally, for example I tried to hack my server to check the vulnerabilities and to patch them. I have experience with tools like Kali Linux, ParrotOS, MITM, SET, Metasploit, Wireshark, Nmap and so on.

Design

This website is an example of my web design skills, when I want to photo shop or make a logo I use pixlr or gimp. For me working in a beautiful desktop environment is a must, left you see my main laptop and my smaller laptop their desktop environment.

Italent


what is Italent?

At school we have a course named Italent, it's a course that helps us knowing ourselves, what we want, who we are and what we are able to do. We analyze this on the base of SWOT analysis, personality tests like MBTI, Ofman, DISC and more . The main focus is discovering our talents and to get the most out of it. Other good things about this course are that we learn to be confident, independent and to build a personal portfolio.
